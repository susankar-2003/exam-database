1. You meet K, L, and M. K says, "We are all knaves." L says, "Exactly one of us is a knight." What is M?
A) Knight
B) Knave
C) Neutral
D) Cannot be determined
Ans: B
2. You meet N, O, and P. N says, "O is a knave." O says, "P is a knave." P says, "N is a knave." Which is a possible combination of knights (T) and knaves (F) for N, O, P respectively?
A) T, T, F
B) T, F, T
C) F, F, F
D) T, F, F
Ans: B
3. You meet Q, R, and S. Q says, "R is a knight." R says, "S is a knight." S says, "Q and R are knaves." How many are knaves?
A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 0
Ans: B
4. You meet T, U, and V. T says, "U is a knave." U says, "T is a knave." V says, "Both T and U are knaves." What is V?
A) Knight
B) Knave
C) Alternator
D) Cannot be determined
Ans: B
5. You meet W, X, and Y. W says, "At least two of us are knaves." X says, "W is a knave." Y says, "X is a knave." How many are knaves?
A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 0
Ans: B
6. You meet Z, A, and B. Z says, "A is a knave." A says, "B is a knave." B says, "Z is a knight." How many are knights?
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) 3
Ans: A

B) Knave

D) Cannot be determined

8. You meet F, G, and H. F says, "G is a knight." G says, "H is a knave." H says, "F and G are different types." What are they?

11. You meet O, P, and Q. O says, "P is a knave." P says, "Q is a knight." Q says, "O is a knave." Which of the following is true?
