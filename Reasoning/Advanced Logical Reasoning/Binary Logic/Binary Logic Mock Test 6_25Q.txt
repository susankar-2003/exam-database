1. Person I says, "J is a liar." Person J says, "K is a liar." Person K says, "Both I and J are truth-tellers." Who is a truth-teller?
A) I
B) J
C) K
D) None
Ans: A
2. You encounter M and N. M says, "N is a liar." N says, "M and I are the same type." What is M?
A) Truth-teller
B) Liar
C) Normal
D) Cannot be determined
Ans: A
3. Person O says, "If Person P is a truth-teller, then I am a liar." What are O and P?
A) O is a truth-teller, P is a truth-teller
B) O is a liar, P is a liar
C) O is a liar, P is a truth-teller
D) O is a truth-teller, P is a liar
Ans: C
4. Person Q says, "At least one of us (Q and R) is a liar." Person R says, "Q is a truth-teller." What are they?
A) Q is a truth-teller, R is a liar
B) Q is a liar, R is a truth-teller
C) Both are truth-tellers
D) Both are liars
Ans: A
5. Person S says, "T is a liar." Person T says, "U is a truth-teller." Person U says, "S is a liar." How many liars are there among them?
A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 0
Ans: B
6. You meet V, W, X. V says, "W and X are liars." W says, "V and X are liars." X says, "V and W are liars." How many are truth-tellers?
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) 3
Ans: B
7. Person Y says, "Z is a truth-teller." Person Z says, "Y is a liar." What can you deduce?
A) Y is a truth-teller, Z is a liar
B) Y is a liar, Z is a truth-teller
C) Both are liars
D) Both are truth-tellers
Ans: C
8. Three people, A1, B1, and C1. A1 says, "All of us are liars." B1 says, "Exactly one of us is a truth-teller." What is C1?
A) Truth-teller
B) Liar
C) Spy
D) Unknown
Ans: B
9. A2 says, "B2 is a liar." B2 says, "C2 is a liar." C2 says, "A2 is a liar." Which statement is true?
A) Exactly one is a truth-teller
B) Exactly two are truth-tellers
C) All are liars
D) All are truth-tellers
Ans: A
10. A detective interviews D1, D2, and D3 about a crime. Only one is innocent, and the innocent person tells the truth (guilty lie). D1 says, "D2 is guilty." D2 says, "D3 is guilty." D3 says, "D1 is guilty." Who is innocent?
A) D1
B) D2
C) D3
D) Cannot be determined
Ans: D
11. In a different town, the guilty person tells the truth and the innocent people lie. There is one guilty person. E1 says, "E2 is innocent." E2 says, "E3 is guilty." E3 says, "E1 is guilty." Who is guilty?
A) E1
B) E2
C) E3
D) None
Ans: B
12. You meet F1 and F2. F1 says, "F2 and I are both truth-tellers or both liars." F2 says, "F1 is a liar." What are they?
A) F1 is a truth-teller, F2 is a liar
B) F1 is a liar, F2 is a truth-teller
C) Both are truth-tellers
D) Both are liars
Ans: B
13. You meet G1, G2, G3. G1 says, "G2 is a truth-teller." G2 says, "G1 and G3 are different types." G3 says, "G1 is a liar." What is G2?
A) Truth-teller
B) Liar
C) Normal
D) Cannot be determined
Ans: B
14. H1 says, "I am a truth-teller." H2 says, "H1 is a liar." H3 says, "H2 is a liar." If exactly one person is a liar, who is it?
A) H1
B) H2
C) H3
D) Cannot be determined
Ans: B
15. Three people sit in a circle: I1, I2, I3. Each person says, "The person to my right is a liar." What is the maximum number of truth-tellers?
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) 3
Ans: 1
16. Five people stand in a line. Each says, "The person in front of me is a liar." The first person says, "I am a truth-teller." How many liars are in the line?
A) 2
B) 3
C) 4
D) 5
Ans: A
17. You meet J1 and J2. J1 says, "We are both liars." What is J2?
A) Truth-teller
B) Liar
C) Cannot be determined

18. K1 says, "If K2 is a liar, then I am a liar." What can you deduce?
A) K1 is a truth-teller
B) K2 is a truth-teller
C) Both are truth-tellers
D) Both are liars
Ans: B
19. L1 says, "L2 is a truth-teller." L2 says, "If L1 is a truth-teller, then I am a liar." What are they?

B) Liar

B) Liar

24. Q1 says, "Exactly two of us (Q1, Q2, Q3) are truth-tellers." Q2 says, "Exactly two of us are liars." Q3 says, "I am a liar." Who is a truth-teller?
A) Q1
B) Q2
C) Q3
D) Q1 and Q2
